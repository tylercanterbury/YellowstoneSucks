Old Faithful

Old Faithful was the first named geyser in Yellowstone.

Anywhere between 1200 and 8500 gallons of water are released when old faithful erupts. That's just under the amount it takes to fill a standard swimming pool.(13,500)

Old Faithful was originally named after a confederate soldier's pet name for his rifle, claiming that if "Old faithful" was fired as frequently as the geyser, the war would've been over within the first year.
